# The 500-Pulse Gunner

**Pairing:** Bombardier Beetle (*Brachinus*) as Pyrotechnician

## The hook
When threatened, a bombardier beetle (*Brachinus*) mixes hydroquinones with hydrogen peroxide inside an armoured reaction chamber and ejects the resulting ~100 °C benzoquinone spray as a *pulsed* jet — high-speed cinematography by Dean, Aneshansley, Edgerton & Eisner (*Science* 248, 1990) clocked the train at roughly 500 pulses per second, the same controlled-burst trick a stage pyrotechnician uses to keep a flame effect re-armable instead of a single dump.

## Visual brief

- **Dimensions:** 1080 × 1350 (portrait, social poster)
- **Palette:**
  - `#0B0A10` — onyx (stage / negative space)
  - `#7A1F0A` — oxidized copper (pronotum red-brown / instrument frame)
  - `#E36B1F` — hot quinone jet
  - `#FFC960` — flame highlight / spark
  - `#9DA4AE` — chitin gray (annotation, leader lines)
- **Mood:** instrumented, hazardous-elegant, calm-combustive
- **Key visual elements:**
  - A profile blueprint of *Brachinus* facing left, head and pronotum drawn as a copper shield, the abdomen tipped by a swivelling turret-nozzle at the rear-right.
  - The signature prop: a strip-style oscillogram below the beetle registering the 500-Hz pulse train as discrete vertical spikes — the pyrotechnician's frequency readout fused with the insect's spray.
  - A single horizontal pulse-train firing rightward from the turret toward the frame edge, each pulse a hot copper-yellow ovoid that fades as it travels.
- **Motion:** one primary motion — pulses spring out of the turret on a cubic ease, march across the frame, and fade just before exit; the oscillogram strip beneath ticks one bright bar in sync with each new pulse. A 4-second loop, ~16 pulses on screen at once to evoke the strobe-capture look of the original 1990 photographs.
