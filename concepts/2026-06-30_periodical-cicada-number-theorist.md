# The Buried Number Theorist

**Pairing:** Periodical Cicada (*Magicicada*) as Number Theorist

## The hook
The seven *Magicicada* species of the eastern United States spend their juvenile lives underground and emerge in synchronized broods on life-cycles of exactly **13 or 17 years — both prime numbers**; the leading hypothesis (Lloyd & Dybas 1966; Yoshimura 1997; Goles, Schulz & Markus, *Complexity* 2001) is that prime periods minimize the rate at which a brood shares an emergence year with shorter-period predators or with neighbouring broods, an evolutionarily stable strategy that composite cycles lose to.

- **Mood:** monastic, exact, patient
- **Key visual elements:**
  - A 100-cell **century ledger** (10 × 10 grid of year numbers, 1–100) centered as the hero. The five multiples of 17 (17, 34, 51, 68, 85) burn vermilion; the seven multiples of 13 (13, 26, 39, 52, 65, 78, 91) glow amber. The shared coincidence year (none in 1–100) is the point — primes barely ever collide.
  - A single stylised cicada silhouette centred over the ledger with **translucent amber wings** so the calendar reads through the venation — the insect literally is the math.
