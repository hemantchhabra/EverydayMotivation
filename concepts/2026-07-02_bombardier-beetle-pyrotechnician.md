# The Pocket Pyrotechnician

**Pairing:** Bombardier Beetle (*Brachinus* spp.) as Pyrotechnician

## The hook
The bombardier beetle mixes hydroquinones with hydrogen peroxide in a reinforced reaction chamber where catalase and peroxidase catalyse an explosive oxidation, launching pulsed jets of ~100 °C benzoquinone spray at 368–735 pulses per second — a mechanism resolved in vivo by synchrotron X-ray imaging (Arndt, Moore, Lee & Ortiz, *Science* 348 [2015]: 563–565).

## Visual brief

- **Dimensions:** 1080 × 1350 (portrait, social poster)
- **Palette:**
  - `#0B0C10` — carbon black (elytra / substrate)
  - `#E86A2A` — pronotum orange (beetle body, warnings)
  - `#F4B12A` — spray flash / muzzle glow
  - `#7A2A18` — scorched carmine (reaction chamber, burn marks)
  - `#D8CBB4` — bone-white steam (labels, plumes)
- **Mood:** exact, exothermic, controlled — a munitions engineer's technical plate.
- **Key visual elements:**
  - A stylised cross-section blueprint of the beetle's abdominal apparatus in profile: paired reservoir + reaction chamber, valve, and nozzle firing right.
  - A strobed pulsed jet stitched across the plate, each puff annotated with cadence and temperature.
  - Draftsman-style annotations: leader lines, `≈100 °C`, `≈500 Hz`, `Brachinini` in small caps.
  - Strong lower-left type block: role tag, italic display title, single-line hook.
- **Motion:** the valve strobes, ejecting a new spring-eased pulse every ~50 ms. Each pulse brightens, drifts right, and cools to a haze — a 5-second loop that dramatises the pulsation frequency.
