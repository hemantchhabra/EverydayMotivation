# The Brainless City Planner

**Pairing:** Slime Mold (*Physarum polycephalum*) as Urban Planner

## The hook
When researchers placed oat flakes at the geographic locations of 36 cities surrounding Tokyo, a single-celled, brainless slime mold (*Physarum polycephalum*) grew a tubular transport network whose efficiency, total length, and fault-tolerance closely matched the Greater Tokyo rail system — published by Tero et al. in *Science* 327 (2010): 439–442.

## Visual brief

- **Dimensions:** 1080 x 1350 (portrait, social poster)
- **Palette:**
  - `#0E1014` — deep ink (substrate / negative space)
  - `#F4C430` — Physarum yellow (the organism itself)
  - `#FFE99A` — tube highlight / pale glow
  - `#E8704A` — oat-flake / city-node accent
  - `#8A8F99` — cool draftsman gray (grid, type secondary)
- **Mood:** quiet, exacting, organic-meets-architectural
- **Key visual elements:**
  - A radial network of branching, slightly-glowing yellow tubes, anchored at small orange oat-flake nodes positioned where the prefectural cities sit around Tokyo.
  - A faint architectural grid underneath — the planner's drafting board.
  - Strong type block in the lower-left: large display title, uppercase ROLE tag, and a single-line hook.
- **Motion:** the tube network grows outward from the central Tokyo node along a spring easing, snapping into each city node in sequence; once formed, a gentle pulse travels along the longest branches. A 5-second loop.
