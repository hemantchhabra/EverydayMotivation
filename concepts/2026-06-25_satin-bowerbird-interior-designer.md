# The Bachelor's Mood Board

**Pairing:** Satin Bowerbird (*Ptilonorhynchus violaceus*) as Interior Designer

## The hook
The male satin bowerbird builds an "avenue" bower — two parallel walls of upright sticks on the forest floor — and decorates its cleared forecourt almost exclusively with **BLUE** objects: parrot tail feathers, jacaranda petals, berries, snail shells, and (in modern environments) bottle caps, pen lids, and drinking straws. Borgia's field experiments (G. Borgia, *Animal Behaviour* 33, 1985) showed that the number and tightness of blue decorations directly predict male mating success: females inspect the forecourt's curation and choose accordingly.

## Visual brief

- **Dimensions:** 1080 x 1350 (portrait, social poster)
- **Palette:**
  - `#0E0F14` — deep forest-floor ink (substrate / negative space)
  - `#1E3FA6` — satin blue (adult-male plumage, hero accent)
  - `#5C82C8` — ultramarine (mid-tone blue chip / jacaranda)
  - `#A8C4ED` — pale sky (lightest blue chip)
  - `#8B5CB5` — violet-iris (the bowerbird's diagnostic eye)
  - `#C9A877` — warm twig (parallel stick walls, cream labels)
- **Mood:** meticulous, considered, designer-portfolio precise
- **Key visual elements:**
  - A top-down "swatch card" arrangement of the forecourt: a tidy grid of blue chips, each tagged with the real object it represents (PETAL, FEATHER, BERRY, BOTTLE CAP, PEN LID, STRAW, GLASS, SHELL, BEAD).
  - Two slender parallel walls of bundled twig running floor-to-ceiling on either side of the grid — the bower's avenue itself, framing the curation like gallery walls.
  - A small satin-bowerbird silhouette perched on a branch above the grid, one wing dipped toward a single hero chip about to land in the empty slot.
- **Motion:** swatch chips snap into the grid one-by-one along a spring easing, ordered loosely darkest-to-lightest; the bird's hero chip lands last with a subtle violet-iris pulse. A 5-second loop.
