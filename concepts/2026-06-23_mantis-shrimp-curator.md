## Concept Title
**"The Curator's Lament: An Exhibition for the Chromatically Limited"**

## The Idea
Mantis shrimp possess 16 types of photoreceptors — humans have only 3. To a mantis shrimp, all humans are essentially colour-blind. This poster imagines a mantis shrimp as a formal museum curator who has spent years curating an exhibition of breathtaking, impossible-spectrum art, only to watch tiny human visitors shuffle past, nodding blankly at paintings they can barely process. The shrimp's expression: deeply professional, quietly exasperated.

### Key Visual Elements

1. **The Curator** — Mantis shrimp in precise biological detail, dressed in a perfectly tailored black blazer. The carapace cycles through iridescent bands: cyan → magenta → gold → UV-suggested violet. Round wire-frame glasses sit on its stalked compound eyes — each lens a multi-faceted jewel. One raptorial claw extended, gesturing toward a painting. Clipboard reads: *"Docent Notes — Exhibition IV: Perceiving the Imperceptible."*

2. **The Paintings** — Three canvases in ornate gold frames. Filled with gradients that argue with each other — neon bands, UV halos, polarised shimmer. Each has a placard:
   - *"Ultraviolet Sonata No. 3 — mixed pigment on linen, 2026"*
   - *"Still Life with Polarised Light (and a shrimp) — oil, UV paint"*
   - *"Sixteen Shades of Red (You Only See One) — acrylic"*

3. **The Visitors** — 2–3 tiny human silhouettes at ~10% frame height, flat and featureless in dark grey. One photographs a painting with a phone. One consults a paper map. Earnest but entirely missing the point.

4. **Wall Placard:**
   > *EXHIBITION IV*  
   > *"Colours in 16 Dimensions"*  
   > *A Guided Tour for the Chromatically Limited*  
   > *— Curated by Dr. O. Stomatopod*

5. **Museum Map (bottom-right):** *UV Gallery | Infrared Suite | Polarised Hall | The Mantis Collection | Gift Shop (Postcards in 3 Colours — Sorry)*

### Style & Rendering Direction

- **Style:** Art nouveau meets 19th-century naturalist scientific illustration, with a contemporary museum-poster sensibility.
- **Line quality:** Fine precise ink outlines on the shrimp; looser gestural strokes on human silhouettes.
- **Lighting:** Museum spotlights cast hard amber pools; the curator glows with faint cool bioluminescent light from within its shell.
- **Typography:** Caslon/Garamond serif for the title; small-caps for placards; typewriter mono for clipboard notes.
- **Decorative motifs:** Border details incorporate abstracted raptorial claw shapes as art nouveau flourishes.

---

### Mood

**Wistful, dry-humoured, quietly magnificent.**

The shrimp is not contemptuous — merely resigned. It has poured its entire perceptual world into this exhibition and knows, with sad grace, that its visitors are doing their sincere best. Rewards viewers who know the biology; works as pure whimsy for those who don't. The poster itself is the final punchline: *the exhibition you are looking at is also one you can barely see.*
